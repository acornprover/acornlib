The per-cover lemma `singleton_open_cover_imp_finite_subcover` is in place, and `singleton_is_compact` is now PROVED (`src/topological_space.ac`). The earlier block — a forward `forall(c) { is_open_cover implies exists(items){...} }` block assembled from the per-cover lemma — does time out, because the final `... = is_compact[T](s)` definitional unfold (matching the cover-dependent existential under the `forall`) overruns proof search; note `empty_set_is_compact` only passes on its cached certificate and also times out under `--force-search`. The fix was to write the proof as an **explicit refutation** instead of a forward assembly: cite the unfolding lemma `is_compact_iff[T](s)`, then `if not is_compact[T](s) { let w satisfy { is_open_cover[T](w, s) and not exists(items){...} }; <cite the per-cover lemma at w>; <restate the existential>; false }`. Skolemizing the negation by hand and citing the per-cover lemma at that witness keeps every sub-goal inside the 5s budget (4 fast searches). This refutation pattern is the likely route past the sibling blockers below (connectedness conjunct extraction, product-map continuity) — both are the same "definitional unfold / higher-order match times out under a forward proof" shape.
